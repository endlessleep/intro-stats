Chapter 1: Descriptive Statistics

📊 Percentiles / Quartiles

Definition: A value such that at least \( p\% \) of observations are less than or equal to it and at least \( (1-p)\% \) are greater than or equal to it.

Methodology

Position: \( \text{position} = p \times n \)

  • If it's not an integer → take the next integer.
  • If it's an integer → take the average of the positions \( pn \) and \( pn+1 \).

Key Quartiles

\( Q1 \) = 25%

\( Q2 \) = Median (50%)

\( Q3 \) = 75%

📊 Mean

Definition: The average value of the data.

\( \bar{x} = \frac{1}{n} \sum x_i \)

📊 Median & Mode

Median

The middle value of an ordered dataset.

Mode

The value that occurs most frequently.

📊 Variance & Standard Deviation

Variance (sample, unbiased): The average squared deviation from the mean, computed using \( n-1 \).

Standard Deviation: The square root of the variance, measuring dispersion around the mean.

Variance: \( s^2 = \frac{1}{n-1} \sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2 \)

Standard Deviation: \( s = \sqrt{s^2} \)

Note

Always use \( n-1 \) for a sample to ensure the standard deviation is unbiased.

📊 Interquartile Range (IQR)

Definition: The difference between the third and first quartile (\( Q3 - Q1 \)), representing the spread of the middle 50% of the data.

\( IQR = Q3 - Q1 \)

📊 Boxplot

Definition: A graphical summary based on the five-number summary: minimum, Q1, median, Q3, and maximum.

  • Whiskers: Values within bounds.
  • Outliers: Values outside the bounds.

📊 Outliers

Definition: An observation that lies far from the rest of the data.

IQR Method

Data is considered an outlier if it falls outside the bounds:

\( Q1 - 1.5 \times IQR \)


\( Q3 + 1.5 \times IQR \)

Z-Score Method

Definition: A measure of how many standard deviations a value is from the mean.

\( z = \frac{x - \bar{x}}{s} \)

Outlier if \( |z| > 3 \)

📊 Skewness

Definition: The asymmetry of a distribution.

  • If Median is closer to Q1 → Right-skew (positive skew)
  • If Median is closer to Q3 → Left-skew (negative skew)

Chapter 2: Probability

📊 Sets & Logic

Basic Operations

Union (\( A \cup B \)): A or B occurs.

Intersection (\( A \cap B \)): A and B occur.

🎯 Logic Connections

Subset (\( A \subset B \)): If A happens, B happens.

  • "At least one" → Union
  • "All" → Intersection
  • "None" → Complement

🎯 De Morgan's Laws

\( (A \cup B)^c = A^c \cap B^c \)


\( (A \cap B)^c = A^c \cup B^c \)

📊 Core Probability Rules

Classic Probability: \( P(A) = \frac{N(A)}{N(S)} \) (or \( \frac{\text{favourable}}{\text{total}} \))
Conditions: \( S \) is finite and outcomes are equiprobable.

Geometric Probability: \( P(A) = \frac{|A|}{|S|} \) (Area / Volume)

Axioms

  • \( P(A) \ge 0 \)
  • \( P(S) = 1 \)
  • Additivity if disjoint.

Complement

\( P(A^c) = 1 - P(A) \)

Use for: "at least one" or "none".

\( P(\text{at least one}) = 1 - P(\text{none}) \)

\( P(\text{none}) = P(\bigcap A_i^c) \)

Union (Addition Rule)

\( P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) \)

Disjoint: \( P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) \)


Always check if disjoint! Disjoint → simple addition. Otherwise → subtract intersection.

📊 Advanced Rules

Combinations & Geometry

Difference (if \( A \subset B \)): \( P(B \setminus A) = P(B) - P(A) \)

Monotonicity (if \( A \subset B \)): \( P(A) \le P(B) \)

Useful Inequalities

\( P(A \cup B) \le P(A) + P(B) \)


\( P(A \cap B) \ge P(A) + P(B) - 1 \)

Discrete Probability

If \( P(s_i) = p_i \), then \( P(A) = \sum_{s_i \in A} p_i \)

📊 Combinatorics (Counting Rules)

Order Matters (Permutations) Order Does NOT Matter (Combinations)
With Replacement \( n^k \)
k choices, n possibilities each time		 \( \binom{n+k-1}{k} \)
Keywords: identical, distribute, allocate
Without
Replacement		 \( P_n^k = \frac{n!}{(n-k)!} \)
Fewer choices at each step		 \( C_n^k = \binom{n}{k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!} \)

📊 Conditional & Independent Probability

Conditional Probability

\( P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} \)

Interpretation: Probability of A knowing B has occurred. Sample space is reduced to B.

Multiplication / Chain Rule

\( P(A \cap B) = P(A|B)P(B) \)


\( P(A_1 \cap A_2 \cap A_3) = P(A_1) P(A_2|A_1) P(A_3|A_1 A_2) \)

🎯 Independence

Definition: \( P(A|B) = P(A) \) OR \( P(A \cap B) = P(A)P(B) \)

Crucial: Independent ≠ Disjoint. Disjoint ⇒ NOT independent (if prob > 0).
If A and B are independent, then (A and B&supc;) are independent, and (A&supc; and B) are independent.

📊 Total Probability & Bayes' Theorem

Total Probability

\( P(A) = \sum P(A|B_i)P(B_i) \)

Intuition: Weighted average using partition \( B_i \).

Bayes' Theorem

\( P(B_i|A) = \frac{P(A|B_i)P(B_i)}{\sum P(A|B_j)P(B_j)} \)

Intuition: Reverse the condition: \( P(\text{cause } | \text{ effect}) \).

🧠 Bayes Deep Dive

The Core Problem: You know \( P(A|B) \) but you want \( P(B|A) \) → Reverse the condition!

  • \( P(B) \) = What you thought BEFORE (Prior)
  • \( P(A|B) \) = How likely A is if B is true
  • \( P(B|A) \) = What you think AFTER (Posterior)

Do not just look at \( P(A|B) \). Compare A if B is true vs A if B is false.

⚠️ Crucial Tips & Common Traps

🎯 Keyword Translations

  • "Given that / Knowing" → Conditional
  • "Update / Information" → Bayes
  • "Sequential draws" → Chain rule (without replacement)
  • "At least one" → Complement
  • "Sequence" → Chain rule

❌ Classic Mistakes

  • Forgetting to divide by \( P(B) \) in conditional probability.
  • Confusing \( P(A|B) \) and \( P(B|A) \). They are NOT equal!
  • Assuming independence blindly.
  • Forgetting the partition denominator in Total Probability.
