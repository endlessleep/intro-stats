Chapter 1: Descriptive Statistics

📊 Percentiles / Quartiles

Definition: A value such that at least \\( p\\% \\) of observations are less than or equal to it and at least \\( (1-p)\\% \\) are greater than or equal to it.

Methodology

Position: \\( \\text{position} = p \\times n \\)

  • If it's not an integer → take the next integer.
  • If it's an integer → take the average of the positions \\( pn \\) and \\( pn+1 \\).

Key Quartiles

\\( Q1 \\) = 25%

\\( Q2 \\) = Median (50%)

\\( Q3 \\) = 75%

📊 Mean

Definition: The average value of the data.

\\( \\bar{x} = \\frac{1}{n} \\sum x_i \\)

📊 Median & Mode

Median

The middle value of an ordered dataset.

Mode

The value that occurs most frequently.

📊 Variance & Standard Deviation

Variance (sample, unbiased): The average squared deviation from the mean, computed using \\( n-1 \\).

Standard Deviation: The square root of the variance, measuring dispersion around the mean.

Variance: \\( s^2 = \\frac{1}{n-1} \\sum (x_i - \\bar{x})^2 \\)

Standard Deviation: \\( s = \\sqrt{s^2} \\)

Note

Always use \\( n-1 \\) for a sample to ensure the standard deviation is unbiased.

📊 Interquartile Range (IQR)

Definition: The difference between the third and first quartile (\\( Q3 - Q1 \\)), representing the spread of the middle 50% of the data.

\\( IQR = Q3 - Q1 \\)

📊 Boxplot

Definition: A graphical summary based on the five-number summary: minimum, Q1, median, Q3, and maximum.

  • Whiskers: Values within bounds.
  • Outliers: Values outside the bounds.

📊 Outliers

Definition: An observation that lies far from the rest of the data.

IQR Method

Data is considered an outlier if it falls outside the bounds:

\\( Q1 - 1.5 \\times IQR \\)


\\( Q3 + 1.5 \\times IQR \\)

Z-Score Method

Definition: A measure of how many standard deviations a value is from the mean.

\\( z = \\frac{x - \\bar{x}}{s} \\)

Outlier if \\( |z| > 3 \\)

📊 Skewness

Definition: The asymmetry of a distribution.

  • If Median is closer to Q1 → Right-skew (positive skew)
  • If Median is closer to Q3 → Left-skew (negative skew)
