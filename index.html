Chapter 1: Descriptive Statistics

📊 Percentiles / Quartiles

Definition: A value such that at least \( p\% \) of observations are less than or equal to it and at least \( (1-p)\% \) are greater than or equal to it.

Methodology

\( \text{position} = p \times n \)

  • Not an integer → next integer.
  • Integer → average of positions \( pn \) and \( pn+1 \).

📊 Mean

Definition: The average value of the data.

\( \bar{x} = \frac{1}{n} \sum x_i \)

📊 Variance & Standard Deviation

Variance: \( s^2 = \frac{1}{n-1} \sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2 \)

Standard Deviation: \( s = \sqrt{s^2} \)

Always use \( n-1 \) to ensure the estimator is unbiased.

📊 Interquartile Range (IQR)

\( IQR = Q3 - Q1 \)

📊 Boxplot

📊 Outliers

IQR Method

\( Q1 - 1.5 \times IQR \)

\( Q3 + 1.5 \times IQR \)

Z-Score Method

\( z = \frac{x - \bar{x}}{s} \)

Outlier if \( |z| > 3 \)

Chapter 2: Probability

📊 Sets & Logic

Operations

Union (\( A \cup B \)): Either A or B occurs (at least one).

Intersection (\( A \cap B \)): Both A and B occur.

🎯 De Morgan's Laws

\( (A \cup B)^c = A^c \cap B^c \)

\( (A \cap B)^c = A^c \cup B^c \)

📊 Probability Axioms & Core Rules

Classic Probability: \( P(A) = \frac{N(A)}{N(S)} \)

Conditions: \( S \) is finite and outcomes are equiprobable.

Complement

\( P(A^c) = 1 - P(A) \)

Use for "at least one" or "none".

Addition Rule (Union)

\( P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) \)

Disjoint: \( P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) \)

📊 Advanced Metrics

Inequalities

\( P(A \cup B) \le P(A) + P(B) \)

\( P(A \cap B) \ge P(A) + P(B) - 1 \)

Set Differences & Monotonicity

If \( A \subset B \):

\( P(A) \le P(B) \)

\( P(B \setminus A) = P(B) - P(A) \)

📊 Combinatorics

Order Matters (Permutations) Order Does NOT Matter (Combinations)
With
Replacement		 \( n^k \) \( \binom{n+k-1}{k} \)
Without
Replacement		 \( P_n^k = \frac{n!}{(n-k)!} \) \( C_n^k = \binom{n}{k} \)

📊 Independence & Conditional Probability

Conditional

\( P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} \)

Chain Rule:
\( P(A \cap B) = P(A|B)P(B) \)

Independence

\( P(A|B) = P(A) \)

If A & B are independent, then (A, \( B^c \)) and (\( A^c \), B) are also independent.

Note: Disjoint ≠ Independent!

📊 Total Probability & Bayes' Theorem

Total Probability

\( P(A) = \sum P(A|B_i)P(B_i) \)

Use when finding the overall probability natively across partitions.

Bayes' Theorem

\( P(B|A) = \frac{P(A|B)P(B)}{P(A)} \)

Use to reverse conditions. It updates your prior \( P(B) \) using new evidence \( P(A|B) \).
